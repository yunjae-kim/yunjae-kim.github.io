Research & Projects

Current and past projects

Thermal Stability Analysis of MOSFETs at Cryogenic Temperatures

Oct 2025- Present

Analysing of temperature dependence in metal-oxide-semiconductor field-effect transistor (MOSFET) performance. When cooled below 298 K, the threshold voltage \(V_{th}\) increases due to Fermi level shift, while carrier mobility \(\mu\) improves from reduced phonon scattering. This creates a zero temperature coefficient (ZTC) bias point where the effects cancel, allowing thermally stable operation without external compensation.

Mentor: Su Huyn Lee — School of Energy, British Columbia Institute of Technology.
Funding: Quantum Leaps Grant - SCWIST.

Effects of Anharmonicity on the Heat Capacity of Elemental Solids at High Temperatures

Nov 2025 - Present

Using classical statistical mechanics and pairwise potential via a Morse potential to model the deviation of heat capacity from the Dulong-Petit limit (\(C_v=3R\)) through a linear anharmonic correction in the form of \(C_v = 3R\mathcal{D}(T)(1 + AT) + \gamma T,\) where \(R\) is the gas constant, \(\mathcal{D}(T)\) is the Debye function, and \(\gamma\) is the Sommerfeld coefficient. Comparison with experimental data shows strong agreement (\(R^2>0.91\)) for several metals with predictions particularly accurate for face-centered cubic lattices.

Mentor: Daniel Stedman — Depertment of Physics and Astronomy, University of British Columbia.

Modelling the Behaviour of Ideal Gases

Dec 2024 - Jan 2025

Small simulation project using Unity and C# to model ideal gas behavior under varying thermodynamic conditions. Used the ideal gas law (\(PV=nRT\)) and the Maxwell-Boltzmann distribution to replicate the random motion of particles. Created a visualization system showing particle dynamics and relationships between state variables.

Collaborator: Christian Marrington — St. Thomas Aquinas Regional Secondary School.
